Quick Start Guide

Open a Topic for Reading

This example shows how to open an article for reading.

  1. Select the desired country from the drop-down list.
  2. See details of the entry by clicking the orange title.

    Entries can be found easily because they are grouped in topics:

    • Entry in country - What formalities are needed to enter a country;
    • Extend stay in country - What formalities are needed to extend the stay of persons in a country;
    • Permit - what permits are needed to visit a specific area;
    • Drive own vehicle - Temporary import of your vehicle, but also insurance, driver's license, etc.;
    • Extend vehicle import - Keep your vehicle in the country for a longer period;
    • Store vehicle while leaving - Store your vehicle in the country while you leave.

    Entries can be assessed easily because within every topic group they are grouped in the following types:

    • Topic summary - summary of the conditions;
    • Document details - a document is a required document such as an international drivers permit;
    • Link to non-commercial information - link to a note with information from an official source such as a government;
    • Link to commercial information - link to a note with information from a commercial source such as a supplier of insurance;
    • Link to traveler feedback - a link connecting to a source of user experience information, often more anecdotal.

    Within the groups entries are sorted on date/time (newest on top). Entries that are no longer current are greyed and at the bottom.

Add a Topic

This example shows how the article The approach to tackling coronavirus in the Netherlands is added to the repository.

  1. Select the desired country from the drop-down list of the start screen.
  2. Select the Add new topic button at the bottom left on the topic screen.
  3. If you haven't signed in to Google a Sign in button will appear at the top of the screen. After you have gone through the dialog the button will change to Signed in.
  4. Select the Topic from the list of relevant topics for travelers. In this example Entry in country is the most appropriate.
  5. Select the type of information you provide from the Entry type list. In this case Non-commercial information is the most appropriate.
  6. Add a Title, for example Policy concerning COVID-19 virus
  7. Add a Description. This step is optional. In this example we use the title of the article The approach to tackling coronavirus in the Netherlands. Don't try to copy the entire article here, but rely on the link (see below) instead. In this way the information remains up-to-date when the content of the link is changed.
  8. Add the Link to more information. This is the link to the article: https://www.government.nl/topics/coronavirus-covid-19/tackling-new-coronavirus-in-the-netherlands
  9. The other fields we can leave empty for this example. Additional fields shown change if the selection of Topic or Entry type changes. In the Detailed Instructions you find additional information about all available fields and their meaning.
  10. Submit the entry by selecting the Submit key at the bottom left of the screen.
  11. Return to the country overview by clicking the Return to country overview button
  12. The entry has been added.
  13. Click the orange title to open it.

Modify a Topic

In this example we will change the word corona to COVID-19 in the Description field of the entry that was created in the previous example.

  1. Select the topic to modify by clicking the orange title.
  2. Click the Modify topic button at the bottom left of the screen.
  3. Edit the description.
  4. Submit the entry by selecting the Submit key at the bottom left of the screen.

Note that you cannot delete entries. If you feel that they are no longer valid change This topic is current (last line) to No. The text will become grey and the entry will go to the bottom of the list. If the entry is no longer relevant it can be deleted by a moderator.
